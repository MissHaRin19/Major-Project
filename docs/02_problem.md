# Problem Statement

Modern pacemakers are part of the Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) and rely on wireless communication for monitoring and updates. This connectivity exposes them to cybersecurity threats.

These devices are resource-constrained and often lack strong security mechanisms, making them vulnerable to attacks that can:
- Distort physiological data
- Block communication
- Mislead medical decisions

Such attacks can directly impact patient safety.

## Motivation

- Increasing number of connected medical devices
- Real-world incidents of device vulnerabilities
- High cost and risk of testing on real devices

## Objective

To design a simulation-based system that:
- Reproduces realistic cyberattacks
- Detects anomalies in physiological data
- Demonstrates mitigation strategies in real time
